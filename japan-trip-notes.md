### 🚽 Gut Health / Constipation Prevention
*Last trip was brutal — don't repeat it. This is a real and common travel problem caused by long flights, jet lag disrupting gut rhythm, diet change (more white rice, less of your usual fiber), dehydration, and holding it because you're busy. Fix it proactively.*

**Pack these — take them starting Day 1:**
- **Magnesium citrate** (400mg capsules) — the best one for travel constipation; osmotic effect draws water into the intestines, very gentle, not a harsh laxative; take 1-2 at night before bed every night of the trip ⭐
- **MiraLax** (polyethylene glycol powder) — mix one packet into any drink; can take daily if needed; tasteless, very effective; widely considered the gold standard for travel constipation
- **Colace** (docusate sodium, 100mg) — stool softener, not a stimulant; safe to take daily as a preventive; combine with magnesium for extra coverage
- **Fiber supplement** — Metamucil packets or Benefiber stick packs; mix into water or juice every morning; psyllium husk adds bulk and keeps things moving
- **Probiotic** (shelf-stable capsules, 10-50 billion CFU) — travel disrupts gut flora; start taking 3-5 days BEFORE the flight; continue daily throughout; look for one with Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium strains

**On the plane (Apr 11-12):**
- Drink water constantly — airplane cabin air is extremely dry and dehydrating
- Skip or limit alcohol on the flight (very dehydrating)
- Walk the aisle every 2-3 hours to keep circulation and gut moving
- Take your magnesium or MiraLax on the plane

**Daily habits in Japan:**
- **Drink water constantly** — Japan tap water is some of the cleanest in the world; drink it freely from the tap, convenience stores have bottles everywhere
- **Morning coffee** — you're already a coffee person; caffeine is a natural gut stimulant; let it work for you every morning before leaving the hotel
- **Miso soup** — you'll encounter it constantly at breakfast spots; it's a natural probiotic, drink it; also very hydrating
- **Don't hold it** — when you feel the urge, find a toilet immediately; Japanese public toilets are everywhere (every konbini, every station), immaculately clean, and equipped with heated bidet seats; there is zero excuse to hold it
- **Keep moving** — you'll walk 15,000-25,000 steps/day, which is actually great for gut motility; the walking itself helps
- **Natto** (if you can stomach it) — fermented soybeans, strong flavor, served at traditional breakfast spots; incredible natural probiotic; optional but genuinely effective

**If it still happens:** Japanese pharmacies (ドラッグストア, drug stores — Matsumoto Kiyoshi is everywhere) carry magnesium supplements, fiber products, and mild laxatives over the counter. Ask for 酸化マグネシウム (sankamaguneishiumu) — magnesium oxide tablets, widely sold, very effective.

### 🎒 Packing Notes (April Tokyo)

- **Layers** — daytime highs ~14-18°C (57-64°F), cool in the morning and evening. A light jacket is required; a packable down is ideal.
- **Rain** — April has some rain. A small compact umbrella or a packable rain jacket is worth bringing.
- **Walking shoes** — you will walk 15,000-25,000 steps per day. Comfort is more important than style.
- **Outlet adapter** — Japan uses Type A (flat 2-pin), same as the US. No adapter needed. But voltage is 100V vs US 120V — check your devices are compatible (most modern electronics say 100-240V on the plug, which is fine).
- **Power bank** — your phone will be your map, translator, camera, and transit card. Bring a good one.

### ✈️ Jet Lag Strategy

- **Day 1 (Sun Apr 12):** Land 4:15pm. Stay awake until **10pm Japan time**. No napping. Get outside, walk around, get sunlight.
- **Days 2-3:** Wake with natural light. Do not sleep past 8am even if you feel terrible. This is the fastest way to reset.
- **First-morning anchor:** Set an alarm for 7:30am on Day 2 (Mon Apr 13) even if you slept poorly. It's worth it.
- **Bonus:** The remote work schedule (1am-10am) is actually easier to adapt to if you naturally run night-owl — you may find the sleep rhythm shifts naturally toward late nights by Week 2.
