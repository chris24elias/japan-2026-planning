# Japan 2026 — Day by Day Itinerary

**Base:** Sotetsu Grand Fresa Takadanobaba, Shinjuku-ku, Tokyo
**Dates:** Sun Apr 12 – Mon Apr 27 (16 days, 15 nights)
**Structure:** 1 week remote work → 1 week PTO · all daytime hours free regardless

---

## Day 1 · Sunday April 12 · Arrive

- Land HND ~4:15pm
- Clear customs, pick up Suica card at Haneda airport
- Keikyu Line → Yamanote Line to Takadanobaba (~50-60 min)
- Check in, drop bags
- Light dinner nearby — Shinpachi Shokudo (Shinjuku, traditional Japanese sets, unpretentious, perfect for a tired arrival) or just 7-Eleven/FamilyMart konbini
- Early night — **goal: stay awake until 10pm Japan time**, do not nap through the evening

## Week 1 — Remote Work Week
*Work Tue–Sat mornings 1am–10am JST · All afternoons and evenings free*
*Pattern: work through the night → sleep until ~2pm → free from mid-afternoon*
*Monday Apr 13 is fully free — work doesn't start until Monday night*

### Day 2 · Monday April 13 · Ease in — Shinjuku area + first tsukemen
*Full free day before work kicks in tonight*

- Morning: Slow start. Streamer Coffee Company or local café near Takadanobaba for coffee + breakfast
- Midday: **Menya Sankai** (Okubo — literally the next neighborhood over from the hotel) — voted best tsukemen 2025, gyokai-tonkotsu broth, thick flat noodles ⭐⭐ — arrive 11:15am before the line builds
- Afternoon: Shinjuku Gyoen (Japanese garden, peaceful, ¥500 entry) — easy walk from Shinjuku Station
- Wander Shinjuku: streets, department store windows, get your bearings
- Evening: **Omoide Yokocho** (Memory Lane, Shinjuku) — narrow alley, yakitori smoke, retro atmosphere; great for early drinks + food
- Night: **Zoetrope** (Nishi-Shinjuku, 10-min walk from hotel) — 300+ Japanese whiskies, owner Horigami will educate you ⭐⭐
- *Note: first work shift starts tonight (Mon → Tue 1am JST). Don't stay too late.*

---

### Day 3 · Tuesday April 14 · Yokohama afternoon
*First work recovery day — easy half-day, close range*

- Work 1am–10am · Sleep 10am–2pm
- 2:30pm: Train to **Yokohama** (~30 min on the Shonan-Shinjuku Line)
- **Yokohama Chinatown** — largest Chinatown in Japan; late lunch/early dinner (dim sum, soup dumplings, baozi) ⭐
- **Minato Mirai** waterfront — Red Brick Warehouse, port views, walkable
- **Cup Noodles Museum** (optional, ~¥500) — make your own ramen flavor, genuinely fun
- Back to Tokyo by 8pm
- Low-key evening — you're on work rhythm now

## Week 2 — PTO · Sightseeing & Day Trips
*Fully free — no work at all, days AND nights · This is your time.*

### Day 9 · Monday April 20 · Kawaguchiko + Shiraito Falls 🗻
*Big day trip — full energy, no work tonight*

- 7:15–7:30am: **Shinjuku Expressway Bus Terminal (Busta Shinjuku)**, 4F — Highway Bus to Kawaguchiko (~2 hrs, ~¥2,000). Book in advance at willerexpress.com or highwaybus.com
- Late morning: **Lake Kawaguchi** — lake shore walk, best Mt. Fuji views from the north shore (Oishi Park); rent a bike if clear
- Lunch: Hōtō noodles (Yamanashi specialty) — thick flat noodles in miso pumpkin broth
- Afternoon: Bus to **Shiraito Falls** (~20 min from Kawaguchi Station) — wide curtain waterfall, stunning in late April ⭐
- Return bus to Shinjuku, back by 7–8pm
- Dinner: **Fuunji** (Nishi-Shinjuku, 8-min walk from hotel) — excellent tsukemen, fast turnover ⭐
- *And you can actually stay out tonight — no alarm, no work.*

> **Weather note:** Check Fuji visibility the morning of. Best visibility is early on clear days.

---

### Day 10 · Tuesday April 21 · Tsukiji breakfast → Kamakura
*Long day — two destinations that cluster south of Tokyo*

- 6:45am: Depart for **Tsukiji Outer Market** (~30 min from hotel)
- 7:00–8:30am: **Tsukiji Outer Market breakfast** — fresh seafood stalls, tamagoyaki, tuna sashimi, uni, clam soup. Arrive early.
- 9:00am: **Hamarikyu Gardens** nearby (optional, ~¥300) — tidal pond, beautiful traditional garden
- 10:30am: Train to **Kamakura** (~1 hr via Yokosuka Line from Shimbashi/Tokyo Station)
- Afternoon in Kamakura:
  - **Kōtoku-in** — the Great Buddha; can go inside for ¥50 extra
  - **Hase-dera** temple — hillside views, good gardens
  - Walk **Komachi-dori** shopping street — snacks, crafts, ice cream
  - **Enoshima** (optional — 20 min further) — small island, sea caves, views
- Late afternoon: Train back to Tokyo
- Evening: Free — grab dinner near the hotel, or Tsukemen Gonokami Seisakusho (Shinjuku, shrimp tsukemen)

---

### Day 11 · Wednesday April 22 · Ginza — Grand Seiko + Sushi + Whisky
*The watch day. Plan to spend real time here.*

- Morning (10am): Head to Ginza
- **Kuro Mame Coffee** (Ginza/Nihonbashi) — black burnt cedar interiors, World Brewers Cup barista, stunning space ⭐
- 11am–1pm: **Grand Seiko Flagship Boutique (Wako Building, Ginza)** — world's largest GS lineup, second-floor lounge, boutique-only models. **Bring your passport.**
- Walk 2 min to **Grand Seiko Boutique Namiki-dori** — compare stock, note exclusives
- Lunch: **Kyubey** (Ginza) — classic Edomae omakase, Oribe set ~¥5,500 (10 courses). *Reserve 2-3 weeks ahead.* ⭐
  - *Alternative:* Sushi Issekisancho (Shinbashi, 5-min walk) — ¥8,800 omakase, exceptional tuna
- Afternoon: Walk Ginza, browse galleries and arcades
- **Jugetsudo** — Kengo Kuma-designed bamboo interior, 160-year-old tea merchant, great matcha stop
- Evening: **Apollo Bar** (Ginza) — quiet jazz bar, strong Suntory collection, vintage bottles ⭐
- *Night is free — can extend the evening as long as you want*

> **Watch note:** Boutique handles tax-free paperwork with your passport. ~10% consumption tax refunded at purchase. Remember to declare at customs on return.

---

### Day 12 · Thursday April 23 · Nakameguro + Shimokitazawa
*Full day in two of Tokyo's best neighborhoods*

- Morning: Train to Nakameguro (~15 min)
- **flour+water** (Nakameguro) — California-style café, opens 8am, excellent breakfast + coffee ⭐
- **Onibus Coffee** (Nakameguro) — restored old Japanese house, roastery downstairs ⭐
- **Nakameguro Canal walk** — Meguro River canal, lush and beautiful in late April
- Lunch: **Toriyoshi** yakitori (walk-in only, arrive before opening) — legendary spot, soboro donburi + chochin ⭐
- Afternoon: Train to **Shimokitazawa** (~10 min)
- Wander: record shops, vintage clothing, great neighborhood energy — no plan needed
- **TAP & GROWLER** craft beer stop — 18 taps, Japanese craft including UCHU Brewing ⭐
- Dinner + evening: Stay in Shimokitazawa for a local izakaya, or head back to Shinjuku/Golden Gai for a Thursday night out

- Evening: **Bar Plat** (Jinbocho) — hidden gem, excellent Ichiro's Malt from Chichibu Distillery; quiet book-district neighborhood
